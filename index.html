The Science of Deception

Sensation vs. Perception

Ahmed Jedidi
21/11/2025

The Core Concept

Sensation (Input)

The raw physical energy (light waves) received by the eye. This is purely biological data collection.

Perception (Output)

The brain's interpretation of that data. The brain uses context, memory, and evolutionary shortcuts to construct reality.

1. Checker Shadow

Illusion of Contrast

The Phenomenon: Square A and B are physically identical.

The Science: Lateral Inhibition. The brain "discounts" the shadow, artificially lightening B to help you identify the object's true color.

2. Rubin's Vase

Figure-Ground Reversal

The Phenomenon: You see faces OR a vase, never both at once.

The Science: Multistability. The brain cannot assign border ownership to two objects simultaneously. It acts as a toggle switch.

3. Müller-Lyer

Depth Perception Error

The Phenomenon: The bottom line appears longer, despite being equal.

The Science: Top-Down Processing. The brain interprets the angles as 3D corners (Room vs. Building) and scales the size based on perceived distance.
